# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Prepared by:** Parth Kute | **Reg No.:** 25BSA10151 | **Branch:** CSE (Cloud Computing and Automation)

---

##  DO's

| # | Do This |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional** — use a clear, appropriate profile photo and your real name on LinkedIn and GitHub. |
| 2 | **Think before you post** — ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or a future employer saw this?" |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings** — set personal accounts (Instagram, Facebook) to private and regularly review who can see your posts. |
| 4 | **Give credit for content** — if you share someone else's work, idea, or image, always tag or credit the original creator. |
| 5 | **Report harassment and abuse** — use platform reporting tools if you witness or experience cyberbullying or threatening content. |

---

##  DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Don't share personal information publicly** — avoid posting your phone number, address, college ID, or daily schedule online. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in or spread misinformation** — always verify news from a trusted source before sharing or commenting on it. |
| 3 | **Don't post in anger** — never send or publish something written in frustration; once it is online, it is very difficult to erase completely. |
| 4 | **Don't accept unknown friend/follow requests** — fake profiles are commonly used for scams, phishing, or data harvesting. |
| 5 | **Don't share your location in real time** — posting "at the airport" or "home alone tonight" can create serious safety risks. |
