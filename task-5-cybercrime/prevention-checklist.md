## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — at least 12 characters combining uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a free password manager like Bitwarden to manage them securely.
- [ ] **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts including email, social media, and banking apps.
- [ ] **Do not click links in unknown SMS or WhatsApp messages**, even if they appear to come from your bank, NPCI, or a government agency — always go directly to the official website or app.
- [ ] **Check website URLs carefully** before entering any personal information — verify "https://" and the exact domain (e.g., `sbi.co.in` not `sbi-login-secure.com`).
- [ ] **Keep your phone and apps updated** — security patches in updates fix known vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.
- [ ] **Avoid using public Wi-Fi** (in cafes, college canteens, or hostels) when logging into bank accounts or making UPI payments.

---

##  Financial & UPI Safety

- [ ] **Remember the golden rule: entering your UPI PIN sends money — it does NOT receive money.** If anyone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive" a refund, prize, or salary, it is a scam. Decline and block the contact immediately.
- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, CVV, or internet banking password** with anyone — not even a person claiming to be from your bank's official customer care line.

---

## Social Media & Account Safety

- [ ] **Do not accept friend or follow requests from unknown accounts**, especially those recently created, with no profile photo, or with very few followers.
- [ ] **Review your app permissions regularly** — many apps request access to contacts, camera, and location unnecessarily. Revoke permissions you did not knowingly grant.

---

##  How to Report Cybercrime in India

| Resource | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | **cybercrime.gov.in** — File an online complaint for financial fraud, harassment, identity theft, or data misuse |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Available 24×7. Call immediately if you have been defrauded — early reporting increases the chance of freezing the fraudulent transaction |
| **Local Police Station** | Visit to file an FIR for serious or repeat offences |

> **Tip for students:** If you are defrauded via UPI, call **1930 immediately** and also contact your bank's helpline. Every minute matters — the faster you report, the higher the chance the transaction can be flagged before the money is withdrawn.
